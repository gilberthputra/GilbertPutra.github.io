NYC YELLOW TAXI

Predicting Tip Amount Based on Hourly Weather Forecast

NOTE : Above picture is taken from and courtesy of inhabitat.com.

This Project ultimately aims to develop Penalised Regression Model for which it has the capacity to predict Tip Amount based on widely available hourly weather forecast to help Yellow Taxi Cab Drivers. Furthermore, 2018 Yellow Taxi (YTX) data taken from Taxi and Limousine Committee (TLC) and Global Hourly Climate (GHC) data from National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) will be analysed. From there, the model will be trained, validated and be utilised to predict 2019 with further critical analysis on its performance. With that said, the model performs approximately 0.3071 for R-squared, 1.007 for MAE and 3.171 for MSE when predicting 2019 data.

Below are the following tasks:

  1. Extract 2018 and 2019 Yellow Taxi Data from New York City Taxi & Limousine Commitee (NYC TLC) page.
  2. Extract Hourly Weather Data from National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) page.
  3. Cleaned and Preprocessed 103 Million Instances of NYC TLC Yellow Taxi by utilising PySpark Architecture.
  4. Cleaned, Preprocessed and Combined NCEI Hourly Weather with the preprocessed NYC TLC Yellow Taxi dataset by utilising PySpark Architecture.
  5. Perform a Preliminary Analysis (GeoSpatial and Features Analysis) on the combined dataset.
  6. Perform Statistical Modelling.

In summary based on the results found from 2018, Taxi Drivers are suggested to pickup from La Guardia Airport between 17:00 and 19:00 as passengers generally Tip more than other areas regardless of the weather. As Tip Amount shows little to no less correlation to weather forecasting. Moreover, the amount of weather data may be insufficient incomparison with NYC TLC Yellow Taxi data as weather was taken from the average of every hour reducing the amount of data. However, the weather data utilised were also taking measurement/reading every 15 minutes which might still be lacking as there is significant data imbalance. Despite this, Ridge Regression performs comparably moderate dealing with this problem although it could still be improved by adding more relevant features or increasing weather readings.
